Love in Rewind

I had him once,
not in promises, not in forever,
but in the quiet way he showed up —
every ping, every concern,
a presence I mistook for permanence.

He left,
not with anger,
but with a silence sharp enough
to slice through my arrogance.

I was nineteen then,
I am twenty-five now.
And yet, with him,
time folds in half.

Hi, I'm Sameeksha.

"Some worship power, some cling to love — I stand at the fault lines where both collapse. I dissect illusions, not to destroy them, but to see what survives."

A seeker of cracks, a collector of contradictions. I write about politics, psychology, poetry and personal finance — sometimes in the same breath, always with caffeine in hand.

I failed exams spectacularly. I wrote poems about the same man twice. I made a guide to surviving India's broken news cycle. And I'm still here, thriving in my own chaotic little way — still trying to survive without turning into a sob story.

Written with caffeine and a mild existential crisis. Still broke, a little bruised, but burning bright.
